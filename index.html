Dopant set

  • Host: SnO₂ (dopants substitute Sn)
  • Fixed dopant: Sb
  • Additional dopants (0, 1, or 2):
Nb Ba Ti Zr Mn Ni Fe Zn

Concentration rules

  • Each dopant concentration varies in 5% steps (relative to Sn sites).
  • Total doping: 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30%

Stability descriptor:

Formation energy (\(E_{\mathrm{form}}\)) is used as a proxy for the thermodynamic stability.

\[ E_{\mathrm{form}} = E_{\mathrm{doped}} - E_{\mathrm{pristine}} - \sum_i n_i \mu_i \]
  • \(E_{\mathrm{doped}}\): total energy of doped structure
  • \(E_{\mathrm{pristine}}\): pristine SnO\(_2\)
  • \(n_i\): number of atoms of species \(i\)
  • \(\mu_i\): chemical potential of species \(i\)

Lower \(E_{\mathrm{form}}\) → higher stability.

Conductivity proxy:

Band gap is used as a proxy for electronic conductivity.

  • Smaller band gap → higher carrier density
  • Predicted using ALIGNN (MBJ)

Band gap classifies materials as metal, semiconductor, or insulator.

5% of Sb show the best stability with a very low band gap.

Sb with Ti, Nb, or Zr co-dopants improve significantly the stability of SnO₂, keeping 5% of Sb with high concentration (25 %) of other dopant gives also the best stability, while saving a zero band gab is zero for many cases.
